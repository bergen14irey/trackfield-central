About Track & Field and I

Track and field is one of the oldest sports known to mankind. It encompasses running, jumping, throwing, and combined events. Whether you're a sprinter, distance runner, or field athlete, there's something for everyone in this incredible sport. I started track and field in 8th grade with Cross Country, moved to High Jump my 10th grade year to join basketball teammates, ending up going to state my junior and senior years, and deciding to continue with track in college, where I was introduced to the Decathlon, which I have been training for ever since.

Visit World Athletics for more information!

Favorite Events

  • High Jump: PR of 6' my junior year, injured for most of senior year, only jumped 5'11". With little practice freshman year of college was able to jump 1.80, which is about the same as senior year. PRd at 1.85m this year.
  • Pole Vault: My main focus last year was pole vault. I went from vaulting high jump heights to becoming an actual vaulter quickly, PR of 3.45 my freshman year. This year I vaulted 3.60m multiple times and was close to 3.75 at the conference multi meet.
  • Javelin: Very different, very fun, had great form in first practice and fell in love, improved 10m from start to end of freshman year, threw 40m in mock meet at beginning of this year and ended my sophomore season with a personal best of 42m.
  • Hurdles: My sophomore year I got hurdles down, I improved my times in the 60m and 110m hurdles significantly. Looking forward to being able to run fast instead of only have good form.
